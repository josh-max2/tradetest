Slide it: cells with a measured dwell split (underlined) re-price live. Fresh arrivals are informed moves — a crypto drift to 20–29¢ near settlement wins only 76% fresh but 96% once stale; weather tails run the same direction (seasoned-in-range is safer, fading to nothing inside 4h). From 16,651 settled 2025 tail markets, 43k state-at-checkpoint snapshots (rebuilt 2026-07-08, independently re-verified): each cell answers "the position is marked here right now with that much time left". Read the new reality: a short marked at 15–19¢ with 4–12h left still wins 87.9%; 20–29¢ with >12h, 76.9% (persistent early elevation is genuine disagreement); 65¢+ under 4h wins only ~2% — the below-50 alert takes those seriously now. Even so, holding beats buying back at essentially every cell: fresh drifts are fairly priced (exit saves nothing, pays spread) and stale drifts are overpriced fear (exit burns our biggest edge) — the asymmetry only ever favors holding.

Methodology — where every number comes from

Backtest universe
16,651 settled Kalshi markets (2025) across the zero-maker-fee families (weather highs/lows/rain, BTC/ETH/XRP/SOL/DOGE dailies, gold/silver/gas/oil) that traded at 1–8¢ with ≥1h to close. Live we trade a validated subset — weather high/low + BTC/ETH daily = 40 series (rain, alt-coins and commodities were dropped for weak/unproven per-sleeve EV). Equal-weighted per market (matches 1-contract-per-market sizing).
Fill simulation
A resting sell order behind a queue of Q contracts fills when cumulative real buyer volume exceeds Q; the fill is scored at that moment's price against the final settlement. No assumed fills, no slippage (a resting limit can't fill worse than its price; post-only can never cross).
Fees
$0 maker fees on every eligible series — enforced in code and verified on live orders. Taker fees are impossible by construction (post-only).
Survival odds (grid above)
P(market settles NO | it traded in a price bucket with a given time-to-close remaining), over the same universe.
Forward projection (Testing tab)
4,000 Monte-Carlo paths of the sizing plan using the audited loss rate (4.43% at −94¢) with correlated weather-region clusters and ~1.1-day settlement cycles. Now known conservative: the 2026-07 correlation study found cross-city busts are independent (corr ≈0), so the real drawdown/doubling is likely better than this projection — see Testing. It is the full-Kelly path and applies only after the edge confirms and sizing scales; the current method is 1 contract.
Live data
Everything on the Live tab comes from the actual account: real orders, real fills read back from the exchange, real settlements. The bot never assumes a fill.

Glossary — for readers new to event markets

Selling YES at 4¢
Taking the position that an unlikely event won't happen. We collect 4¢ now; if the event doesn't happen we keep it, if it does we pay $1 (lose 96¢). One contract = $1 at stake.
Maker / post-only
Our orders rest in the order book waiting for a buyer, like a limit order — never paying the spread, never paying fees on these markets. "Post-only" means the exchange rejects the order rather than letting it execute immediately.
Queue ahead
How many contracts were resting at our price before us. Buyers consume the queue in order, so a small queue fills fast; a 100k queue essentially never reaches us.
Settlement
Each market resolves within ~a day (e.g., the day's official high temperature). Winners are paid automatically; there is no exiting early in this strategy — historical data shows cutting losses always costs more than holding.
Fill-conditional EV
Average profit per contract measured only on orders that actually filled — the number that decides whether the strategy scales up.
