Testing — model vs real money

Projected = forward performance implied by the audited backtest (16,651 markets, 2025) + Monte-Carlo of the sizing plan. Actual = performance from real trades with real money — nothing modeled.

The strategy, for someone with zero context: a bot places passive "maker" sell orders on extreme longshots (1–8¢ outcomes) in Kalshi's zero-fee weather/crypto/commodity event markets — economically, selling lottery tickets to buyers who systematically overpay for them. Positions are held ~1 day to settlement. Historically ~95.6% settle in our favor for a few cents each; ~4.4% lose big (−94¢) — the edge is the premium collected exceeding the rare losses, with $0 fees (verified live) and no exit slippage (never sells early).

How to tell if it's working: (1) on the Actual view, the observed loss rate stays ≤6% and EV per fill ≥ +1¢ as settled trades accumulate; (2) the bold real-equity line tracks inside the model's shaded band. Sizing only scales up through pre-defined gates if those hold.
What we expect now (2026-07-05): we're in 1-contract validation — the goal right now is to prove the edge is real, not to grow capital. The full-Kelly doubling plan on the Projected tab is paused and gated on that proof.

The live data is running hotter than the model so far: —. Normalized to 1 contract, the whole book to date is roughly flat, so the edge has not yet separated from noise. Realistically, expect flat-to-thin P&L in the near term while enough settled trades accumulate (~a few more weeks) to tell whether the true edge is the modeled +1¢/fill or whether the low sleeve drags it toward breakeven. Doubling only begins once the edge confirms and sizing is re-enabled.

One correction to the model, one real caveat: the Monte-Carlo below assumed correlated weather clusters, but we since proved cross-city busts are independent (correlation ≈0, clustering matches pure chance) — so the ~63-day / 29%-drawdown projection is if anything pessimistic, and breadth diversifies better than modeled. The genuine residual risk is narrower: the low sleeve (busts several times more than highs, and may cluster on a broad cold snap — untested) plus crypto (BTC/ETH correlated, capped).
