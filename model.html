Testing — the method & whether it's working

The method right now is a validation, not a growth plan. We run 1 contract per market to prove the edge is real on real money. The doubling projection lower down is the future plan — paused and gated on that proof. Live results is the real-money track record, nothing modeled.

The strategy, for someone with zero context: a bot places passive "maker" sell orders on extreme longshots (1–8¢ outcomes) in Kalshi's zero-fee weather event markets — economically, selling lottery tickets to buyers who systematically overpay for them. Positions are held ~1 day to settlement. Historically ~95.6% settle in our favor for a few cents each; ~4.4% lose big (−94¢) — the edge is the premium collected exceeding the rare losses, with $0 fees (verified live) and no exit slippage (never sells early).

How we tell if it's working: on the Live results view, the observed loss rate holds ≤6% and EV per fill turns clearly ≥ +1¢ as settled trades accumulate toward the confirming sample. Sizing only scales up — through pre-defined, evidence-based gates — once that holds.

The correction the model needs — cross-city busts are independent

The projection below was Monte-Carlo'd assuming correlated weather clusters. We since proved cross-city busts are independent (pairwise correlation ≈0; day-to-day clustering matches pure chance) — so the projected doubling and its ~29% drawdown are if anything pessimistic, and breadth (40 series) diversifies the risk better than modeled. The genuine residual risk is narrower than the model's: the low sleeve (busts several times more than highs, and could cluster on a broad cold snap — untested) plus crypto (BTC/ETH move together, capped at 20%).

The ceiling — how big this can get if it works (capacity-audited)

The bottleneck is taker flow, not our capital — the thin 3–8¢ books only trade so many contracts a day, and they empty near the 14:00 UTC open. Sizing the fills we could realistically capture across 40 series caps deployment at roughly $11–19k of collateral (it recycles daily) earning ~$145–240/day at the modeled ~1.3% ROI. Past that, extra capital just sits idle — you can't force more fills into books that aren't trading. Every number here assumes the edge holds; that's what stage 1 is testing.
